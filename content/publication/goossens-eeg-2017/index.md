abstract: We investigated the power of EEG as biomarker in differential diagnosis
  of Alzheimer's disease (AD) and frontotemporal lobar degeneration (FTLD). EEG was  recorded
  from 106 patients with AD or FTLD, of which 37 had a definite diagnosis,  and 40
  controls. Dominant frequency peaks were extracted for all 19 channels, for  each
  subject. The average frequency of the largest dominant frequency peaks  (maxpeak)
  was significantly lower in AD than FTLD patients and controls. Based on  ROC analysis,
  classification could be made with diagnostic accuracy of 78.9%. Our  findings show
  that quantitative analysis of EEG maxpeak frequency is an easy and  useful measure
  for differential dementia diagnosis.
